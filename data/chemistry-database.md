# Chemistry database

## Analytically Reduced Chemistry (ARC) for hydrogen combustion at atmospheric pressure, including NOx chemistry

The chemical kinetics results from a complex reduction process from the detailed CRECK mechanism [1] with an appropriate NOx sub-mechanism from Ref. [2]. The final ARC mechanism comprises 12 transported species (N2, H2, H, O2, O, H2O, OH, HO2, NO, NO2, N, NH), 4 species in Quasi-Steady State (QSS) (N2O, HONO, NNH, NH2), and 38 reactions. More details are given in the [supplementary material](https://ars.els-cdn.com/content/image/1-s2.0-S1540748924000889-mmc1.zip) of Ref. [3].

To use this mechanism: [Cantera cti](/assets/data/ARC_S12R38QSS4.cti), [Fortran kinetics](/assets/data/ARC_S12R38QSS4.f90)

[1] Ghobad Bagheri et al. "Comprehensive kinetic study of combustion technologies for low environmental impact: MILD and OXY-fuel combustion of methane", *Combustion and Flame*. [10.1016/j.combustflame.2019.10.014](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.combustflame.2019.10.014)

[2] Yu Song et al. "The sensitizing effects of NO2 and NO on methane low temperature oxidation in a jet stirred reactor", *Proceedings of the Combustion Institute*. [10.1016/j.proci.2018.06.115](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.proci.2018.06.115)

[3] Quentin Malé et al. "The LEAF concept operated with hydrogen: Flame topology and NOx formation", *Proceedings of the Combustion Institute*, 2024. [10.1016/j.proci.2024.105278](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.proci.2024.105278)
